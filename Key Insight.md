Key Insight
1. Student Performance Distribution
Most students achieved average scores within a moderate range, indicating that the overall academic performance is balanced with only a few exceptionally high or low performers.

2. Study Hours and Academic Performance
Students who spend more time studying generally tend to achieve higher average scores. This suggests a positive relationship between study hours and academic success.

3. Gender-Based Comparison
The difference in average scores between male and female students is relatively small, suggesting that academic performance is not strongly influenced by gender in this dataset.

4. Correlation Between Variables
Correlation analysis indicates that study hours and attendance have positive relationships with average scores. Students who study more and attend classes regularly are more likely to achieve better results.

5. Presence of Outliers
A small number of students have exceptionally high or low scores compared to the rest of the dataset. These observations may represent outstanding performance, learning difficulties, or possible data anomalies.

6. Consistency in Performance
Most students show relatively consistent performance levels, with only a few cases displaying extreme variations in academic achievement.

7. Areas for Improvement
Students with low study hours and limited study hours tend to have lower average scores, indicating potential areas where academic support and intervention may be beneficial.

8. Overall Conclusion
The analysis suggests that study habits and study hours are important factors influencing student performance. Encouraging consistent attendance and effective study practices may help improve overall academic outcomes.

Report-Ready Conclusion
The data analysis revealed that academic performance is positively associated with study hours. Students who dedicate more time to studying and maintain regular study hours generally achieve higher average scores. While demographic factors such as gender show minimal impact, behavioral factors appear to play a significant role in determining student success. Additionally, a few outliers were identified, representing either exceptional performance or unusual observations that may require further investigation. These findings highlight the importance of consistent academic engagement in improving student outcomes.
